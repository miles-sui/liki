# About 25 Types

Not better, not worse — just the shape of who you are.

## What Is 25 Types

25 Types is built on the Five Elements — Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water. But unlike most personality systems, it doesn't put you in a box. It maps you into a continuous space where your personality is a shape, not a label. Every position in that space means something.

The framework maps you to one of 25 archetypes: 5 pure types dominated by a single element, and 20 composite types where two elements shape each other. Your type is a direction, not a boundary. It describes tendencies, not limitations.

## The Five Elements

The Five Elements have been around for over 2,000 years. They've lasted because they describe something real — how energy moves, grows, and balances itself. Wood (growth, initiative), Fire (warmth, expression), Earth (stability, connection), Metal (structure, refinement), Water (depth, reserve). These are not literal substances. They are dynamic tendencies — patterns in how people engage with the world.

Two fundamental cycles connect them:

- **Sheng** (nourishing cycle): Wood fuels Fire, Fire enriches Earth, Earth forms Metal, Metal carries Water, Water feeds Wood. Each element brings forth the next.
- **Ke** (restraining cycle): Wood penetrates Earth, Earth absorbs Water, Water quenches Fire, Fire melts Metal, Metal cuts Wood. Each element keeps another in check.

These cycles are not "good" or "bad." They are forces — directional, contextual, natural. Throughout the site, we use words like *nourish*, *restrain*, and *facilitate* to describe these dynamics. They are chosen deliberately to convey direction without value.

When we compute the Bond between two people, we quantify these forces. When someone's Wood feeds your Fire, that is not a compatibility score — it is a description of what happens when your shapes meet.

## Your Shape, not Your Score

Most personality assessments give you a label and stop. 25 Types gives you a **deviation vector** — a five-dimensional quantity called d. Each of its five components represents how much you deviate from the average on that element, positive or negative. The components sum to zero, which means your shape is about internal balance, not absolute level.

Think of it as a silhouette. A person who leads with Wood and has quieter Water has a distinct outline. Another person, with elevated Fire and less prominent Metal, has an entirely different one. Both are complete. Neither is "higher" than the other.

There is also a **proportion vector** p, used for display. All five components are positive and sum to 1. It shows the relative prominence of each element in you, like a pie chart of your tendencies. But the real work happens with d — the shape that describes your internal dynamics.

## The 25 Archetypes

The 25 archetypes emerge naturally from the geometry of the five-element space:

- **5 pure types**: one element clearly dominates. The Pioneer (Wood), the Luminary (Fire), the Steward (Earth), the Refiner (Metal), the Reservoir (Water).
- **10 Sheng-pair composites**: two elements that nourish each other, in either order. Wood-Fire is different from Fire-Wood — the leading element matters.
- **10 Ke-pair composites**: two elements that restrain each other. Wood-Earth is different from Earth-Wood. The dynamics are distinct.

The boundary between types sits at a 42-degree angle on the unit sphere — a mathematical choice that distributes the archetypes approximately evenly. Most people are composites. That is expected, not exceptional.

## Why It Is Different

Five things set 25 Types apart from other personality frameworks:

**Zero parameters.** The entire model — the 25 prototype positions, the Bond computation, the Flow projection — contains no tunable coefficients. No weights to adjust. No thresholds to tweak. Every number you see is a direct consequence of your measured deviation vector d and the known S and C matrices that encode the nourishing and restraining cycles.

**Linear algebra, not statistics.** The framework rests on permutation matrices and vector geometry, not on statistical curve-fitting to population samples. The S and C matrices are fixed — they are the same today as they will be in ten years. Your result depends on your choices, not on a training dataset.

**Native interaction arithmetic.** Because every profile is a vector in the same five-element space, you can compute the dynamic between any two people directly. Bond is not a bolted-on compatibility meter — it is the natural operation of the S and C matrices on two deviation vectors. This is uncommon in personality models.

**Flow — a seasonal dimension.** Your effective shape shifts across the twelve solar months. The solar terms (Jieqi) are an astronomical calendar based on the sun's position, not a lunar or astrological system. Flow shows when your elements strengthen and when they recede — a personalized energy cycle grounded in the same mathematics.

**Evaluation without judgment.** The framework describes; it does not rank. No type is "better." No Bond outcome is "recommended" or "avoid." The words we use — nourish, restrain, facilitate — are chosen deliberately to convey direction without value. Your shape is not a score. It is the shape of who you are.
