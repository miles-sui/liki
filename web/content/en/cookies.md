# Cookie Policy

This policy explains how 25 Types uses cookies and similar technologies. Unlike most websites, we keep it short — because we use almost none.

## What Are Cookies

A cookie is a small text file that a website stores in your browser. Cookies serve many purposes: keeping you logged in, remembering your preferences, or — on many sites — tracking your activity across the web.

## Cookies We Use

We set a single cookie: a **JWT (JSON Web Token)** for authentication. This is an `httpOnly` cookie that identifies your logged-in session. It is set when you log in and cleared when you log out. No other cookies are set by our domain.

## What We Do Not Use

We do not use any of the following:

- Advertising cookies
- Third-party analytics cookies (no Google Analytics, no tracking pixels)
- Social media cookies or widgets
- Browser fingerprinting
- Cross-site tracking of any kind
- Session replay or heatmap scripts

This means you will never see a "25 Types cookie consent banner" demanding you configure dozens of tracking categories — because there is nothing to consent to for tracking. The one cookie we set is strictly necessary for the service to function.

## Third-Party Cookies

Our payment provider may set session cookies on its own domain during checkout. These are subject to the payment provider's cookie policy, not ours. Our email delivery provider does not set cookies on our domain. No other third-party cookies are present.

## Cookie Duration

Our JWT cookie expires after 30 days (matching the token's validity period). If you log out, the cookie is removed client-side and the token is invalidated server-side. If your session is inactive beyond the token's lifetime, you will need to log in again.

## Managing Cookies

You can disable cookies in your browser settings. However, disabling our authentication cookie means you cannot log in or use account-related features. The free assessment, type exploration, and all public content remain available without cookies.

Most browsers allow you to clear cookies for specific sites without affecting others. Instructions vary by browser — check your browser's help section for details.

## Legal Basis

Under the GDPR, ePrivacy Directive, UK GDPR, and similar regulations worldwide, our authentication cookie qualifies as a "strictly necessary" cookie. It is exempt from consent requirements because the logged-in service cannot function without it. If you choose not to log in, no cookie is set at all.
